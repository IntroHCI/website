Assignment 5, Example 2

List of Changes

  • The navigation bar is now persistent throughout the app to address our issue of a confusing navigational hierarchy.
  • We implemented a more fleshed out goal timeline in order to address issues with being able to access a global list of goals.
  • We visibly tag each milestone now with color (associated with a certain goal) and a description of goal progress to address confusion about which milestone belongs to which goal. We also have the option of filtering the timeline to show one goal's milestones at a time.
  • We ditched the graph/chart concept and we're now going strictly with a timeline. This allows us to decrease the complexity of goal and milestone creation, as we no longer need to have the user input a lot of quantifiable data in an effort to extrapolate their progress. The removal of graphs should address a lot of usability concerns raised in the heuristic evaluation process, and has the side benefit of pushing our app towards minimalism.
  • In conjunction with the above change, we're removing the quantifiable/unquantifiable distinction and have users input all of their goals and milestones as plain text, tagged with a due date, simplifying the goal and milestone creation process.
  • Tapping on a milestone reveals information about the goal it's associated with and gives further options for viewing the goal and editing/deleting goals and milestones.
  • Excuses will now be visible on the timeline, in place of the milestone you missed. This solves the problem of being unable to see your past excuses and gives the user a more holistic view of their path to goal completion.
