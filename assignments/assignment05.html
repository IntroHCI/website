Assignment 5: Skeleton and a plan

Assignment

Revisit the Heuristic Evaluation

Start by taking a look at the feedback you got last week from the Heuristic Evaluations of your prototypes. Distill the HE results into a list of concrete changes you want to make to your prototype. Use what worked, and improve what didn't. If you haven't already, make a decision about the prototype you are going with. This could be one of the two you tested or a combination of them. Again, take the time you need : this is what you will be working on for the rest of the course, so make sure you will feel invested in it.

Make a Development Plan

Create a detailed and comprehensive development plan for how your interactive prototype will be built. The development plan will guide your work for the next few weeks - but be sure to fill out as much as you can for the remaining weeks in the quarter. Write down all the different components of your prototype. You can view a component as one chunk of the interaction that has a deadline for when it should be done. Pay attention when you define the chunks, it should be something concrete enough for your peers to assess if it has been completed. "Set up results page" is too vague. "Display train times on results page" is. Use a Google spreadsheet or something similar for your development plan. You are free to structure the plan as you like, but if you'd like some hints this template is for you. Thoughtfully structure deadlines--don't have everything due Thursday night every week!

From your development plan it should be clear exactly what needs to be done, and when it will be done. Be sure to plan so that the prototype is done in time for user testing. When you are done, you should have a solid feeling for your interface and be ready to move into programming.

Your plan should also list one or two outside constraints. These could include deadlines or exam dates for other classes, work, travels, or anything that will be most important for you to account for over the next few weeks.

Create a Home Screen & Key Links

Create a home screen and add links that lead to the major screens. It's okay if the screens are placeholders; the goal is to get a navigational skeleton up there that someone can click around and get a good feel of the application's flow. Make sure that if you have a button or icon, it links to something, whether itâ€™s an implemented page or simply a holding page. You will fill in the blanks and flesh out the details later. This is a minimal requirement for the building part of the assignment. You are encouraged to go beyond.

Thinking Ahead

We know that many of you have midterms or major projects this week, so this week's assignment is lighter than the next two weeks' assignments. We strongly encourage you to use this time to start working on the functionality of your prototype. It is in your own best interests to get started on the functionality as soon as possible.

Evaluation criteria & Grading rubric

Category Nope Adequacy Proficiency Mastery
List of Changes
3 points		 No changes or completely irrelevant changes. The student only identified a few changes from the heuristic evaluation feedback and a large amount of feedback is ignored in the prototype; the changes would introduce some HE violations. Many of the simpler suggested changes were made, but some of the more complex or difficult issues were not addressed; the changes would not introduce any obvious HE violations. The user made several insightful and specific changes based on the heuristic evaluation feedback. It is hard to find any HE violations at all with the changes taken into account.
Development Plan
3 points		 No development plan or completely irrelevant plan. The student's development plan does not address every step of development and does not create clear actionable tasks. The development plan has several reasonable steps for development, but they are not clearly defined or do not cover all aspects of development. The development plan has many distinct, logical steps that give a clear path for development.
Deadlines
3 points		 No deadlines or completely irrelevant deadlines. The student's timeline seems haphazard and the deadlines are obviously impossible to follow. The student's timeline is well-organized and mostly doable, although a few of the deadlines seem idealistic or unreasonable. The timeline is well-organized, has feasible deadlines, and takes into account time for unforeseen issues.
Navigational Skeleton
3 points		 No home screen or navigational skeleton. Home screen has little content, or navigation does not work. The home screen appears to have most of its content, and the major navigations are present. Home screen and navigational skeleton are very thorough and well planned. The navigational skeleton gives a real feel for the flow of the application and is clearly thought through.

Outside the Box
+1. Up to 5% of submissions.		 The changes based on the heuristic evaluation are not only insightful and specific, but show creativity and thought in the changes that are made.
